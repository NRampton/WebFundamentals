Changing your own oil


Ever wanted to learn to change the oil on your car? I'd recommend looking here. But if you're not going to heed that timely warning, read on, friend.

Here are your instructions:

  1. Get the front of your car off the ground. Use a jack, jack stands, be safe. You're going to be underneath your car. If it teams up with gravity against you, you're going to lose.
  2. Look under the car and find the oil drain plug. You're looking for a bolt pointing at the ground on a slant, attached to a flat area, like this.
  3. Put a drain pan under that bolt, then remove the bolt. This will be messy.
  4. Once the old oil is gone, put the drainage bolt back and change the oil filter:
    1. Unscrew the old filter. Could be tough, may need an oil filter wrench.
    2. Dump the old oil out of the filter.
    3. Put a little oil on the gasket of the new filter and screw it on hand-tight.
  5. Now pour in new oil. Make sure it's the right kind (see table below) and the right amount. Check your owner's manual for this info.
  6. Dispose of the old oil properly. Municipal recycling facilities are a good bet, or your local auto parts store.
