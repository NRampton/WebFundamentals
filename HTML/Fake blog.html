Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn (Dec 1918–August 2008)

Aleksandr Isayevich Solzhenitsyn was a Nobel Prize-winning author and memoirist. Famous for writing about his prison experiences in works such as The Gulag Archipelago, and for facilitating Krushchev's efforts to discredit Stalin's regime after the latter's death.

Key Events in Solzhenitsyn's Life

  • 1918: Born in Kislovosk
  • 1945: While serving in WWII, arrested for comments made about Stalin in letters to a friend.
  • 1953: Ends term of imprisonment, sent into internal exile in Kazakhstan.
  • 1962: Publication of One Day in the Life of Ivan Denisovich.
  • 1974: Expelled from the Soviet Union.
  • 1994: Returns to Russia.
  • 2008: Dies of heart failure in Moscow.
